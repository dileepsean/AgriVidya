Explain psychrometrics for food engineering exam
Psychrometrics is the study of thermodynamic properties of moist air — crucial for GATE AG and food drying! Key terms: Dry Bulb Temperature (DBT), Wet Bulb Temperature (WBT), Relative Humidity (RH%), Dew point, Specific humidity (kg water/kg dry air), Enthalpy (kJ/kg). Used in: spray drying, freeze drying, grain storage, cold store air conditioning. The psychrometric chart plots all these relationships visually. About AgriVidya
Built by Engineers, for Engineers

AgriVidya is India's dedicated AI-powered learning and guidance platform for Food Engineering, Agricultural Engineering and Food Science. We exist because students in these fields deserve the same quality of AI tools and exam resources that students from top institutions have access to — regardless of where they study.

Our platform is built by specialists in Food Engineering, Agricultural Engineering and AI — with deep understanding of the Indian university system, GATE syllabus, ICAR requirements and agri-sector career pathways. Every tool, every piece of content, every explanation is crafted with one goal: to give engineering students the most accurate, exam-focused, syllabus-aligned guidance available.

We believe that a student at a state agricultural university in Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Maharashtra or any part of India deserves the same depth of learning support as a student at IIT or IARI. AgriVidya is our commitment to making that possible — through technology, through AI, and through a genuine passion for food and agricultural engineering.
